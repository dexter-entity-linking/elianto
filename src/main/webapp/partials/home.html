Welcome to the Dexter Entity Linking Annotator

Entity Linking allows to automatically detect entities in documents.


We are asking your help in generating a human annotated dataset.

What is an Entity?

An entity could be a person, a place, an event or a material thing. They are things of interest to users of the web who are either searching for information (e.g., is there a good Italian restaurant in San Francisco?).

Usually people refer to an entity using one of its names: we call these mentions spots . Do you know that there are more than 50 different spots that people use to refer to the Berkley University ? ( u.c. berkley, cal berkley, berkley university, ucb, university of california...).

Unfortunately words have a million different meanings, and spots too. If someone says "I love boston", she probably means the big city in Massachusetts, but she could be also referring to one of the 26 other bostons around the world, or to the boston band.

How can you help us?

We are trying to create a collection of documents manually annotated with spots and correct entities, to evaluate and improve how our automatic approaches are good to annotate a document. We ask you to contribute to the collection annotating just a few documents. Your contribution will help the research on search engines and information retrieval. Let's provide people with better access to the Internet knowledge!

How does it work?

Have a look at our annotation guidelines and to the FAQ page. Then perform the login (using google/facebook, we will keep only your mail) and contribute to our project annotating some documents! Thank you!
